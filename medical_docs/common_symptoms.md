# Common Health Symptoms and When to Seek Medical Care

## Fever

Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often due to an illness. A fever is usually a sign that your body is fighting an infection.

### Normal Temperature Ranges:
- Normal body temperature: 98.6°F (37°C)
- Low-grade fever: 99-100.4°F (37.2-38°C)
- Moderate fever: 100.4-102.2°F (38-39°C)
- High fever: Above 102.2°F (39°C)

### When to Seek Medical Care:
- Temperature above 103°F (39.4°C)
- Fever lasting more than 3 days
- Fever with severe headache, stiff neck, confusion, or difficulty breathing
- Fever with persistent vomiting
- Signs of dehydration

### Home Treatment:
- Rest and drink plenty of fluids
- Use over-the-counter fever reducers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen
- Cool compress on forehead
- Wear light clothing

## Headaches

Headaches are very common and can have many different causes. Most headaches are not serious, but some require medical attention.

### Types of Headaches:
1. **Tension Headaches**: Most common type, feels like a tight band around the head
2. **Migraines**: Intense, throbbing pain, often with nausea and light sensitivity
3. **Cluster Headaches**: Severe pain around one eye, occurring in clusters
4. **Sinus Headaches**: Pain in forehead, cheeks, and around eyes due to sinus inflammation

### Red Flag Symptoms (Seek Emergency Care):
- Sudden, severe headache unlike any before
- Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or rash
- Headache after head injury
- Headache with vision changes, weakness, or difficulty speaking
- Headache that worsens over time

### Home Treatment:
- Rest in a quiet, dark room
- Apply cold or warm compress
- Stay hydrated
- Over-the-counter pain relievers
- Regular sleep schedule
- Stress management

## Cough

Coughs are a common symptom that can be caused by various conditions, from simple viral infections to more serious respiratory issues.

### Types of Cough:
- **Dry Cough**: No mucus production
- **Productive Cough**: Produces mucus or phlegm
- **Acute Cough**: Lasts less than 3 weeks
- **Chronic Cough**: Lasts more than 8 weeks

### When to See a Doctor:
- Cough lasting more than 3 weeks
- Coughing up blood
- Difficulty breathing or wheezing
- High fever with cough
- Chest pain
- Cough interfering with sleep
- Weight loss with chronic cough

### Common Causes:
- Viral infections (common cold, flu)
- Bacterial infections
- Allergies
- Asthma
- Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
- Postnasal drip

### Home Remedies:
- Stay hydrated
- Use a humidifier
- Honey (for adults and children over 1 year)
- Throat lozenges
- Elevate head while sleeping
- Avoid irritants like smoke

## General Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain or pressure
- Sudden severe headache
- High fever (over 103°F/39.4°C)
- Persistent vomiting
- Signs of severe dehydration
- Sudden confusion or altered mental state
- Severe abdominal pain
- Uncontrolled bleeding

Remember: This information is for educational purposes only and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult with healthcare providers for proper diagnosis and treatment.
