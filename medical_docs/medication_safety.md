# Medication Safety and Information

## General Medication Safety Guidelines

### Before Taking Any Medication:
- Always read the label carefully
- Check expiration dates
- Understand the correct dosage
- Know potential side effects
- Check for drug interactions
- Inform your doctor about all medications you're taking

### Common Over-the-Counter Medications

## Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
**Uses**: Pain relief, fever reduction
**Dosage**: Adults: 325-650mg every 4-6 hours, maximum 3000mg per day
**Warnings**: 
- Do not exceed maximum daily dose
- Avoid alcohol while taking
- Can cause liver damage if overused
- Check other medications for acetaminophen content

## Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
**Uses**: Pain relief, fever reduction, inflammation
**Dosage**: Adults: 200-400mg every 4-6 hours, maximum 1200mg per day (OTC)
**Warnings**:
- Can cause stomach irritation
- Take with food
- Avoid if you have kidney problems
- Can increase blood pressure
- May increase risk of heart attack or stroke

## Aspirin
**Uses**: Pain relief, fever reduction, heart attack prevention (low dose)
**Dosage**: Varies by use (81-325mg for heart protection, 325-650mg for pain)
**Warnings**:
- Not for children under 18 due to Reye's syndrome risk
- Can cause stomach bleeding
- Blood thinner effect
- Avoid before surgery

## Antihistamines (Benadryl, Claritin, Zyrtec)
**Uses**: Allergies, hay fever, hives
**Common Types**:
- Diphenhydramine (Benadryl): Causes drowsiness
- Loratadine (Claritin): Non-drowsy
- Cetirizine (Zyrtec): May cause mild drowsiness

**Warnings**:
- Can cause drowsiness (especially Benadryl)
- Avoid alcohol
- Can interact with other medications

## Prescription Medication Safety

### Important Points:
- Take exactly as prescribed
- Don't share prescription medications
- Complete full course of antibiotics
- Don't stop medications suddenly without consulting doctor
- Store medications properly
- Dispose of unused medications safely

### Common Prescription Categories:

**Antibiotics**:
- Take full course even if feeling better
- Don't save leftover antibiotics
- Can cause stomach upset
- May reduce effectiveness of birth control

**Blood Pressure Medications**:
- Take at same time daily
- Don't skip doses
- Monitor blood pressure regularly
- Don't stop suddenly

**Diabetes Medications**:
- Monitor blood sugar levels
- Be aware of hypoglycemia symptoms
- Maintain consistent meal schedule
- Adjust for illness or stress

## Drug Interactions

### Common Interactions to Avoid:
- Alcohol with pain medications
- Blood thinners with aspirin
- Certain antibiotics with dairy products
- Some medications with grapefruit juice
- Multiple medications containing acetaminophen

### How to Prevent Interactions:
- Keep an updated medication list
- Use one pharmacy when possible
- Inform all healthcare providers about medications
- Read labels carefully
- Ask pharmacist about interactions

## Side Effects and When to Call Doctor

### Common Side Effects:
- Nausea or stomach upset
- Drowsiness
- Dizziness
- Headache
- Skin rash

### Serious Side Effects (Call Doctor Immediately):
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe allergic reaction (hives, swelling)
- Chest pain
- Severe stomach pain
- Unusual bleeding
- Severe mood changes
- Signs of liver problems (yellowing skin/eyes)

## Medication Storage

### Proper Storage:
- Store in cool, dry place
- Keep in original containers
- Away from children and pets
- Check temperature requirements
- Don't store in bathroom (humidity)
- Keep medications in dark places

### Disposal:
- Don't flush medications
- Use medication take-back programs
- Remove personal information from labels
- Mix with unpalatable substance if no take-back available

## Emergency Situations

### Call 911 if experiencing:
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Difficulty breathing
- Loss of consciousness
- Chest pain
- Severe bleeding
- Suspected overdose

### Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:
- Consult doctor before taking any medication
- Avoid certain medications during pregnancy
- Some medications pass through breast milk

### Elderly:
- May require lower doses
- Higher risk of side effects
- More likely to have drug interactions
- Regular medication reviews important

### Children:
- Doses based on weight, not age
- Different formulations available
- Never give adult medications to children
- Use appropriate measuring devices

Remember: This information is educational only. Always consult healthcare professionals for medication advice specific to your situation.
